# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single hobbyist user (developer) browsing the first 50 Pokémon (Gen I) fetched live from PokéAPI. Used casually, at a desk, in a browser — not a mobile-first or high-frequency tool. The job to be done: quickly scan, search by name, and sort a Pokémon roster by height/weight/type.

## Product Purpose

A small reference app that fetches Gen I Pokémon data and presents it as a searchable, sortable two-column table. Success is a fast, legible browsing experience that feels like it belongs to the Pokémon world rather than a generic admin table.

## Brand Personality

Playful Pokédex. Bold, saturated color, a bit game-UI: Pokédex-screen energy, type-color accents, rounded shapes, tactile hover/press states. Confident and fun, not corporate.

## Anti-references

Avoid the generic SaaS/AI-generated look: no gradient text, no tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows, no cream/beige/parchment "warm neutral" body background, no side-stripe card borders, no glassmorphism-as-decoration. Avoid making it look like an internal enterprise data-grid tool — it should feel like it belongs to Pokémon, not to a B2B dashboard.

## Design Principles

- Game-world confidence over corporate restraint — bold color and shape choices are on-brand here, not a risk.
- Clarity of data first — search and sort are the core interactions; playfulness must never reduce table legibility or scan-ability.
- One committed accent strategy, not scattered type-color chaos — Pokémon type colors are used deliberately (e.g. in icons/tags), not smeared across the whole UI.
- Small surface, high craft — there are only two components (title/search, table), so both must be polished to a high standard rather than spreading effort thin.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA contrast (≥4.5:1 body text, ≥3:1 large text/UI elements). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for any hover/focus/transition effects. Search input must be fully keyboard-operable with a visible focus state.
